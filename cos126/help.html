HELP

The COS 126 staff is very happy to help students review curriculum and work on assignments. We have set up several methods for you to reach us, we recommend taking advantage of as many of these as you wish. It's a tough class!

Have a question? Post it to Piazza!

Perhaps the most important tool for this class is Piazza, an online forum where you can post a question annonymously and get an answer from the course staff rather quickly. In fact, our average response time is only ten minutes! Note that questions posted after midnight may not be answered until the following day. We encourage students to make Piazza an integral part of their course experience. Keeping up with the questions other students ask can be a great review tool.

Office Hours & Lab TA's

We are lucky to have a runs plenty of office hours if you'd like to get help in person. We will gladly look at your code or review old exams with you. You're welcome to attend any staffmembers' office hours, regardless of who your preceptor is. The computer science department also hires upperclassmen COS majors as undergraduate teaching assistants to supplement office hours.

Warning. Our schedule may change. Please double check this page before going to office hours.

I Missed My Precept!

Worry not my dear soul. If you missed your precept, or you are planning to miss a precept, you can attend any other COS 126 precept to get the same instructional content (and avoid falling behind in the course!). We run the same content twice a week. All the Tuesday and Wednesday precepts are interchangable, same with all Thursday and Friday precepts. All we ask is that once you've picked a replacement precept, email both preceptors as soon in advance as you can to ensure that everyone knows what's going on and both precepts run smoothly.

Need more help? We offer One-On-One Review Sessions & Peer Tutors

The nature of the curriculum in 126 is cumulative, such that if a student falls behind, they will need to first catch up with what they missed in order to understand the concepts currently being covered. If you find yourself falling behind, be proactive. Contact your preceptor as soon as possible to schedule a one-on-one meeting to review the material. It is the job of the preceptor to be available for his or her students that need review.
